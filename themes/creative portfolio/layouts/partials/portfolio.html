Retail activity change

The first activity reports plot shows the evolution of retail affluency during the pandemic. We relate them with vaccinations, covid19 cases and Lockdown measures. Everything is scaled according to covid-19 cases. They activities are measured as increase or decrease in percentage of volume of affairs.

A timeline is remarked where it can be interactively rendered, with their description at the bottom, the diferent relevant events and periods that distinguished the pandemic cicles and it social impact with their associated restrictions. [Timeline of UK lockdowns] Anyway minor restrictions went on for periods out of the lockdowns, clearly shown by many trends.

Some relevant insights:

# TOREWRITTE Starting from a similar average activity in all sectors, we can see the peaks prior to confinement in food (where the population was supplied by these -especially toilet paper as reported in the media-) and the beginning of the down in the rest of the activities. Weekend peaks are also observed, especially in ‘Social’. Then there is the drastic decline in all activities with the imposition of the first confinement, with the most affected sectors being reservations for restaurants and and ‘social’, and a less drastic change for food and pharmacy products. It is also observed that while during the confinement the most affected sectors remain stationary with very low activity, as the confinement progresses if trade in general, both food and recreational, is recovering, remaining with a significantly higher activity. elevated food and basic necessities. With the lifting of the first confinement, all sectors are exploring a linear recovery during the summer, but without having recovered the activity of pre-pandemic 'normality', if several points -outliers- are observed, probably due to the holiday periods and back to work. Grocery stores are ahead of the rest of the sector. Given the restrictions applied with the second September wave, the two most punished sectors 'social' and 'restaurant reservations' return to low levels of confinement, and trade and recreation also fall, while grocery and pharmacist products do not experience in this case any downfall. effectiveness of lockdowns. Giving a first look of data seem that the first 2 lockdowns didn't helped that much, but seeing the trend of the second (great increase rate) stopping the increasing curve at the beginning it's fundamental. Moreover, we know that "an effective reduction in transmission must have occurred nearly immediately after the tighter lockdown, given the lag time of around 10 days from asymptomatic infection to diagnosis." Taking that into account we can clearly see that after a small time lag infections started to decrease [Lockdown timing and efficacy in controlling COVID-19] Taking a look into activities behavior we can clearly see that, for instance, social econimcal activities had very few volume of affair during lockdowns, due to strict regulamentations of customers number and take-away only. This category is clearly the one that covid had the worse impact to, reducing retail volume dramaticaly for the whole restriction periods. Contrarywise, we can see that grocieries and pharmacies after an initial drop in sales volume remained active with a slightly increasing rate. It's important to notice a huge drop in thei sales in late december, obviously related to Christmas days. We can clearly see that during weekends we have a decreasing in cases number, this is due to the fact that "testing is not functioning seven days a week, unlike Covid-19". [Covid-19 cases and the weekend effect] 5) Another interesting fact is that London (and the whole Great Britain actually) faced much more cases in the lastCovid-19 wave. It's difficult to say why. According to what Time : "experts say [...] the crisis is a result of both the struggle to deal with a new variant estimated to be up to 70% more transmissible, and because of a failure in decisive and strong government leadership. " We can infact clearly see that cases dropped after the last hard restrictions took place. [Third wave of covid in Great Britain] Least, but not last It's interesting to highlight that decreasing in retail/moving are not directly following covid cases but more follow the restrictions. And that decrease actually stopped the virus. This again shows how much restrictions are important since people without law enforcement do not realize the importance of not spreding the virus.
